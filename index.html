What Is a Zero-Knowledge Proof (ZKP) and Why Should You Care?
(Originally from Batch 11, #2)

Basic idea:
A ZKP lets you prove that you know something — say, the password to a wallet, or that a transaction was valid — without revealing the details of what you know.

Example:
Imagine proving you’re over 18 without showing your birthday. A ZKP can do that — it proves the logic of the claim (age > 18) without exposing personal info.

Why it matters:

  • Privacy: Hide wallet balances, transaction amounts, or identity — while still proving legitimacy.
  • Scalability: Bundle thousands of transactions into one proof (used in zk-rollups) and post it on Ethereum or another Layer 1.
  • Security: Prove off-chain computation was done correctly — think AI inference, or financial data pipelines.

Recursive SNARKs = Superpowers:
Recursive proofs let you nest ZKPs, so one small proof can verify thousands of others — like compressing a 10,000-page report into a single verified summary.

Tools:
Zcash, Polygon zkEVM, Mina Protocol
