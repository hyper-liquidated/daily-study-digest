The Social Layer

The Paradox of Choice Revisited
Journal of Experimental Psychology: General, 2024 Summary:

Revisits the famous 'jam experiment' and finds that fewer choices consistently result in faster decisions and greater satisfaction.

Why it’s notable:

Strong real-world implications for marketing, UI design, and productivity systems.

The Inheritance Lottery: How Family Wealth Shapes Risk-Taking and Innovation
Quarterly Journal of Economics, 2024 Summary:

Shows unexpected inheritances increase entrepreneurship — but only among top-wealth households.

Why it’s notable:

Fresh look at wealth inequality’s influence on ambition and innovation.

How Cities Shape Mental Health: Noise, Density, and Access
Lancet Public Health, 2023 Summary:

Analyzes mental health outcomes across 30+ cities. Green space and access to services reduce anxiety, while noise and isolation increase risk.

Why it’s notable:

Links urban policy and mental health — with strong implications for zoning, noise control, and walkability.

The Scarcity Mindset Revisited: Cognitive Load or Learned Helplessness?
Nature Human Behaviour, 2024 Summary:

Re-examines the effect of financial scarcity on decision-making, suggesting a learned helplessness framework over bandwidth tax.

Why it’s notable:

Challenges a popular behavioral theory with new empirical evidence.

Algorithmic Nudges for Civic Participation
PNAS, 2023 Summary:

Shows that simple email nudges increase voter turnout and civic activity more effectively than social media campaigns.

Why it’s notable:

Applies behavioral nudging to real-world democracy-building with strong statistical backing.

Architectures of Capital

Rollups & Data Availability: Why Scaling Isn’t Just About Throughput
Ethereum Foundation, Celestia, StarkWare blogs Summary:

Rollups batch transactions, but without guaranteed data availability, they risk censorship and broken trust.

Why it matters:

This underpins Ethereum's future. Data availability is crucial for modular security and scaling.

Applications:

zkSync, Celestia, EigenDA

Tools:

Proto-Danksharding, RollCall

Latency Arbitrage and the Hidden Economics of Speed
Budish, Cramton & Shim (2015 + 2023 follow-ups) Summary:

High-frequency traders exploit milliseconds for profit — at the cost of efficiency.

Why it matters:

Reinforces need for exchange reforms like IEX, MEV protection in DeFi.

Applications:

Fair market design, batch auctions

Tools:

CowSwap, Flashbots, Jito

Collateral Rehypothecation in DeFi Lending Markets
Gauntlet, 2024 Summary:

Explores how collateral is re-used across protocols (Aave, Compound) and the systemic risk it creates in a liquidity crunch.

Why it matters:

Shows how leverage chains form even without margin lending — risk modeling for DeFi is still immature.

Applications:

Collateral efficiency, protocol insolvency design

Tools:

Gauntlet risk simulations, Chaos Labs

Stablecoin Peg Risk: When “1:1” Isn’t Enough
Chicago Booth Working Paper, 2023 Summary:

Studies how even fully collateralized stablecoins can break peg due to bank run dynamics, price path dependency, and liquidity exit games.

Why it matters:

Important for anyone modeling stablecoins, risk curves, or “flight-to-safety” dynamics in tokenized cash equivalents.

Applications:

Stablecoin audits, DAO reserve policies

Tools:

Curve, Frax, Maker risk dashboards

Intent-Based Architectures: From Order Flow to Strategy Flow
Flashbots Research, 2024 Summary:

Explores how protocols like Anoma and SUAVE are shifting from transaction-based block building to intent-level execution.

Why it matters:

Big shift in how DEXs and wallets will route trades — less MEV, more privacy, and strategy-aware settlement.

Applications:

Wallet routing, private execution, DEX UX

Tools:

Anoma, SUAVE, CoW Protocol intents
