Study: Rollups & Data Availability: Why Scaling Isn’t Just About Throughput
(Originally from Batch 1, #3)

1. Reframed Summary (Plain-English Version):

What it’s about:
This study explores how rollups (Layer 2 scaling solutions) compress many blockchain transactions into one, but without guaranteed data availability, users can’t trust what was actually committed.

Why it matters:

  • Data availability is what makes rollups secure, censorship-resistant, and decentralized.
  • Protocols like Celestia and EigenDA are solving this by separating data availability from execution.
  • This unlocks secure, scalable infrastructure for Ethereum and beyond — without compromising decentralization.

Main takeaway:
Rollups aren’t truly scalable unless users can independently access and verify data. This paper shows how data availability is central to the future of modular blockchains.
